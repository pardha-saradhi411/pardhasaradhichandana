Technical Stack:

Inventory Management is built upon a robust technical stack to deliver a reliable and scalable solution:

Frontend & Backend:

PHP Laravel:

Laravel's Blade templating engine and frontend capabilities are leveraged to develop the frontend interface, providing a seamless integration between backend and frontend components. Utilized as the backend framework, Laravel offers a powerful and elegant solution for building web applications, providing features such as routing, authentication, and database interactions.

MySQL Database:

Employed for data storage and retrieval, MySQL offers a reliable and scalable solution for managing relational data within the Inventory Management application.

Inventory Laravel

Inventory Management is a comprehensive web application developed as part of a server-side web programming course, aimed at facilitating efficient management of assets, owners, and personnel within an organization. With a focus on usability and functionality, Inventory Management offers a range of features to streamline inventory tracking and user management processes. Users can manage assets, owners, and personnel through functionalities such as addition, editing, deletion, and retrieval of information. Specifically, administrators have the capability to add, edit, delete, and view assets, owners, and personnel records, ensuring seamless management of inventory-related data.
